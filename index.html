bio

My research studies applied social science questions for economic development often with a quantitative approach (social network analysis, experimental design, bayesian modeling, and regularization). I have a BA in mathematics and economics and a PhD in agricultural and resource economics. My research topics are situated in behavioral and development economics, utilizing lab experiments in the field, randomized control trials (RCT), and quasi- randomized designs examining how social networks and group behavior affect the take-up of new agricultural technologies. My current work builds on this by testing the framing of information with regards to resource management, risk reduction and climate, some via web applicationas and information communication technologies that I've built.

My secondary work is in regularization methods (ridge regression) and the inverse problem that address the noisy and highly correlated data or ill-conditioned design matrices, relevant to econometrics, image and sound recovery, and big data.

I believe in making technology and STEM accessible and free. To this end I co-run Pyladies NYC, have taught for Girls Who Code, and regularly host and teach workshops on programming and data science. In my spare time I amm automating watering my plants with a raspberry pi and sensors. I was also once a professional ballet dancer with sfb.
